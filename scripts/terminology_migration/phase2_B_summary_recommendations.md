## Category 2: Context-Specific Adaptations

### 2a. Survival/Persistence Contexts
Original: "survive without a living host plant"
- **For herbivores:** "survive without a living host"
- **For predators:** "survive without prey"
- **For detritivores:** "survive without suitable substrate"
- **Generic:** "survive without a suitable host or food source"

**Lines:** 497, 1840, 1881, 2512, 3711

### 2b. Cultivation/Production Contexts
Original: "host plants are cultivated" / "produced in greenhouses"

These are plant-pest specific. For terrestrial invertebrates:
- **Keep agricultural examples** for species affecting crops
- **Add ecological alternatives** like "hosts occur in natural habitats"

**Lines:** 1448, 1565, 1632, 1642, 1839, 1880, 1920, 1965, 2108, 2129, 2170, 2207, 2246, 2371, 3652, 3657, 3694

### 2c. Distribution/Occurrence Contexts
Original: "host plants are widespread" / "host plants grow"

**Replacement:** "suitable hosts or habitats are widespread" / "occur"

**Lines:** 1490, 1510, 1546, 1556, 1576, 1586, 1610, 1653, 1686, 1697, 2324, 2325, 2462, 3186, 3192, 3827

### 2d. Spread Rate Contexts
Original: "host plants' occurrence does not limit spread"

**Replacement:** "host or habitat availability does not limit spread"

**Lines:** 2406, 2440, 2462

## Recommended Approach

### Option A: Minimal Changes (Conservative)
Replace only:
- `host plant(s)` → `host(s)` or `suitable host(s)`
- `host plant commodity` → `host material or commodity`

This preserves the document structure and most examples.

### Option B: Comprehensive Adaptation
- Rewrite plant-specific examples to include:
  - Herbivore examples (host plants)
  - Predator examples (prey populations)
  - Detritivore examples (deadwood, leaf litter)
- Update cultivation references to include natural habitat occurrence

### Option C: Hybrid (Recommended)
- Simple replacements (Option A) as baseline
- Add parenthetical clarifications where needed
- Add brief notes for non-plant contexts
